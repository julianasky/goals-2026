Goal 01 Org design
Enable portfolio operating model reset — I lead my tribe leads to deliver the right outcomes for the right type of work
Team leadership Strategic trade-offs Exec alignment
Outcome
I have led my tribe leads to operate with the right model for their type of work — making hard structural trade-offs, removing blockers, and creating the environment for timely delivery of the right business outcomes. Execs understand what Enable is accountable for, how to engage with it, and trust it as a dependable partner. Each tribe lead is performing against a clear mandate with the autonomy and support to succeed.

Major initiatives

Make hard structural calls and get exec buy-in
  • Identify tribes where the current model is not fit for purpose and bring a clear structural recommendation — not just an observation — to exec level
  • Make the hard trade-offs: what gets investment, what gets rationalised, what changes ownership; don't let ambiguity persist
  • Secure exec endorsement of the new operating model so tribe leads have the authority and air cover to operate within it
Lead tribe leads to operate with the right model
  • Set distinct expectations per tribe based on function type — product strategy and outcomes-based roadmaps where appropriate; service contracts and SLAs where not
  • Actively remove blockers and work around constraints that prevent tribe leads from delivering — don't just identify them
  • Invest in the growth of tribe leads: ensure they have the context, skills, and support to lead their teams to strategic results
Create a dependable operating rhythm for the Enable space
  • Own the meta-backlog for Enable: the prioritised set of bets across tribes that reflects what matters most for Skyscanner right now
  • Provide a cadence of clear, regular updates to execs and stakeholders — with honest signal on where we are ahead, on track, or at risk
  • Manage cross-tribe dependencies proactively so they don't become emergencies

Definition of done

  • Structural decisions endorsed at exec level, not just documented
  • Each tribe lead has a clear mandate, appropriate operating model, and is performing against it
  • Enable meta-backlog owned, prioritised, and visible to execs on a regular cadence
  • Blockers and cross-tribe dependencies actively managed — not left to escalate
Goal 02 AI practice
AI-native ways of working — I set the direction for how Skyscanner's product, engineering, and CX disciplines build together with AI
AI-powered growth Voice of the practitioner Company-wide practice
Outcome
I have autonomously identified and led Skyscanner's response to an unmet, previously unaddressed need: how product, engineering, and CX actually work together when AI is in the loop. I have set the direction, used AI itself to accelerate discovery and insight generation, and created the conditions for this to become sustained company-wide practice — not a one-off initiative. Skyscanner is raising its bar on AI-native product development because of this work.

Major initiatives

Lead discovery — and use AI to accelerate it
  • Autonomously design and lead discovery across product, engineering, and CX — surfacing how teams build with AI today, what's working, and what the biggest unaddressed gaps are
  • Leverage AI for rapid synthesis, pattern identification, and insight generation from discovery — model the behaviour we're trying to build
  • Bring findings and a clear point of view to execs and product leadership — not just data, but a recommended direction with trade-offs named
  • Guard against bias and quality gaps in AI-assisted workflows: surface where AI is creating false confidence or eroding craft
Set the direction for AI-native product development at Skyscanner
  • Define an ambitious, long-term point of view on what AI-native collaboration between product, engineering, and CX looks like — and what it demands of each discipline
  • Turn that point of view into testable hypotheses and iterate quickly: pilots, experiments, documented outcomes
  • Make hard calls about what good looks like and what we will stop accepting — this is direction-setting, not facilitation
  • Represent Skyscanner's thinking on AI-native product development in external forums where relevant
Create the infrastructure for sustained company-wide practice
  • Lead a cross-discipline community of practice — set its direction and ensure it has enough momentum to outlast any single initiative
  • Build cross-pollination mechanisms that connect Loyalty, Geo Growth, and platform tribes so learnings travel at pace
  • Measure adoption of new practices, not just satisfaction: are teams actually changing how they work, and is it producing better outcomes?

Definition of done

  • Discovery completed using AI-accelerated synthesis; exec-level point of view shared and acted on
  • A long-term direction for AI-native ways of working endorsed and adopted beyond a single team
  • Community of practice self-sustaining with clear signal of practice change — not just attendance
  • Measurable improvement in how teams across product, engineering, and CX collaborate with AI in the loop
Goal 03 Risk & alignment
P0 platform health — I drive peer alignment across capability functions to move Skyscanner's highest-risk shared infrastructure gaps from known risks to funded reality
Peer influence Stakeholder management Exec escalation
Outcome
I have proactively identified platform health risks that no single capability function could resolve alone, and driven the peer alignment needed to act on them. Working alongside the product directors for Shared Products and Data as equals — building shared understanding, surfacing the right trade-offs to execs, and refusing to let P0s die in planning cycles — I have moved the most critical gaps from known risks to funded, in-flight initiatives with clear owners and measurable progress.

Major initiatives

Proactively convene peers — don't wait to be asked
  • Initiate the conversation with peer product directors in Shared Products and Data to jointly surface the highest-risk gaps: disaster recovery, data sovereignty, security posture, AI-scale infrastructure resilience
  • Bring a consistent prioritisation framework to the table — likelihood, blast radius, regulatory exposure, dependency on AI, Loyalty, and Geo Growth — so the three functions can align on a shared list, not three separate ones
  • Produce a joint recommended P0 list and bring it to exec level with a unified business risk case — not three separate asks from three separate functions
Build the peer relationships that make alignment possible
  • Invest in strong working relationships with the Shared Products and Data product directors — understand their constraints, roadmaps, and incentives, not just our own
  • Navigate disagreements on prioritisation and ownership with honesty and pragmatism — find the shared ground rather than defaulting to each function protecting its patch
  • Escalate to exec level jointly where needed — a unified voice from three capability functions carries more weight than three separate escalations
Establish a durable cross-capability operating rhythm
  • Co-create a lightweight governance model with peers — a forum that produces decisions, not just status updates
  • Ensure P0 progress is visible to exec leadership on a regular cadence, framed in business risk terms
  • Make this rhythm sustainable so it outlasts any single initiative and becomes the normal way the three functions manage shared platform health

Definition of done

  • A joint P0 list agreed across Enable, Shared Products, and Data — not three separate lists
  • P0 list endorsed at exec level with a unified business risk case from all three capability functions
  • Every P0 has a funded delivery plan and unambiguous owner — none are still sitting on a list
  • Cross-capability governance rhythm co-owned with peers and producing decisions, not just updates
Goal 04 AI velocity
Building with AI — I set the long-term vision and lead this cross-functional workstream to measurably faster, safer, AI-native development
AI-powered growth Product strategy Workstream leadership
Outcome
I have set an ambitious, long-term strategy for how Skyscanner engineers build with AI — and led this cross-functional workstream to deliver against it. I own the meta-backlog for Building with AI, make the hard prioritisation calls between competing capabilities, and hold the bar on quality, governance, and bias prevention. The North Star of engineering time saved is no longer aspirational: it is measured, improving, and credible to exec leadership.

Major initiatives

Set the long-term strategy and own the meta-backlog
  • Define an ambitious multi-horizon strategy across AI platform capabilities: LLM engineering, ModelOps, agentic platform, AI-ready knowledge, and security guardrails
  • Own the meta-backlog: make the hard trade-offs between competing capabilities and communicate those decisions clearly to the tribe and to execs
  • Define business success in measurable terms — engineering time saved, AI Coverage Rate, Time to Self-Serve — and hold the tribe accountable to them
  • Partner with engineering leadership so the strategy is co-owned and funded, not just endorsed in principle
Lead the workstream to measurable, fast-learning delivery
  • Create the conditions for fast iteration: POCs, MVPs, and rapid feedback loops that validate hypotheses before scaling investment
  • Remove blockers and manage dependencies that slow the workstream — don't wait for engineers to escalate
  • Inspire workstream contributors to strategic results: set high expectations, celebrate learning from failure, and hold the bar on quality
  • Ensure the golden path is real: opinionated, well-supported, and actually used — not a reference architecture that nobody follows
Guard against bias, quality gaps, and ungoverned AI
  • Close the governance gap that exists in the current KPI framework — define what governed AI use looks like, measure it, and make it a first-class concern
  • Proactively identify where AI tooling is creating quality gaps, bias, or false confidence in engineering workflows — and act before they become incidents
  • Ensure cost guardrails (cost per 1K LLM sessions, token usage, platform reliability) are monitored and enforced as adoption scales
  • Build governance into the golden path from the start — not as a compliance gate bolted on after

Definition of done

  • Multi-horizon strategy set and endorsed by exec leadership — not just shared
  • Meta-backlog owned with hard trade-offs documented and visible
  • Engineering time saved baseline established; leading indicators tracked monthly and improving
  • Governance KPI defined, instrumented, and reported to AI LT at QBR
  • Workstream delivering at pace against strategy — not just building; learning and iterating quickly
